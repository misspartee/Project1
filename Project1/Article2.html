To promote quality in UX/UI design there are fundamental skills that you should acquire. First is user advocation, as a designer you will be the true advocate for the user. To do this you need to ask users what they like and what they don't like. Second is virtual communication. Virtual communication is how you communicate the design ideas in your head. This comes in handy when explaining your ideas to project managers and developers. Lastly, familiarize yourself with different digital design software. With digital design software you can create icons, logos, and interface mock ups. Photoshop, Sketch, and Adobe Creative Suite are most commonly used. While I liked this article, a lot of the information was obvious. I would recommend this article to UX/UI designers who are new to the trade.
